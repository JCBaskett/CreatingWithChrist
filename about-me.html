About Me

My recovery journey from domestic abuse in Liverpool, has involved exploring my personal heritage and Liverpool’s legacy of ingenuity and slavery, out of which, in the autumn of 2022, I developed a creative business selling lavender microwaveable hearts and running recovery workshops. Alongside this I oversee an online creative community with a vision to set up an emporium connecting people to a global network of creatives, to educate about ethical and sustainable art, appropriating the nature and essence of people and resource in different parts of the world. I am learning Victorian crafts: sewing, painting, printing, interior design, and woodwork.

I use fabric and paint to capture colour and essence of people and nature. Inspired by William Morris and 100 years of peace which enabled stability and ‘building to last’ by the Victorians, I work with individuals to uncover their ‘beauty and function’, recognising our internal state affects what we create. A novice in my skills, new to creative art, I am moving to recording that beauty and function on an individual and collective level, seeking to redeem history and restore destiny though art and pros, locally and internationally. My passion is seeing people/groups become who they truly are. 

My passion is redeeming the past, in a bid to build an international network of creatives and connect people to ethical designs and makes out of Liverpool.
